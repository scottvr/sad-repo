Key design decision
A task update = ~96 coefficients over frozen random rank-4 bases (K=8 per site, 12 sites on distilgpt2). Composition is exact addition and reversal exact negation in parameter space, so all observed forgetting/irreversibility is behavioral interference through the frozen network — the thing the hypothesis is actually about. This also distinguishes the setup from plain LoRA/task-arithmetic (argued against LoRAHub/AdapterFusion/task vectors in the notes, per your novelty clause).

Findings: single tasks fit perfectly in 96 dims; superposition in a shared basis fails hard (the cosine² penalty didn't save it); separate subspaces (LoRA stacking) compose better but reverse dirtily; context routing recovers ~90–100% of performance from held-out context phrasings. Bonus ablation: the KL drift anchor cuts neutral-probe drift 12× (5.31→0.45) and makes routing perfect, but doesn't fix superposition interference. Paraphrase consistency is 0.0 everywhere — these tiny updates memorize phrasings, not semantics.

Incomplete / caveats
Single seed, no error bars; ortho/gates/K ablations designed but not run; gradient-free fitting and replay-free retention losses are documented next steps; run_baselines.py is implemented and --help-verified but its exact artifact is subsumed by sequence_eval.json.

Most promising next step: the routed-vs-composed gap is the story — run multi-seed ablations (--ortho sweep, K sweep 4→32) to test whether any cheap control makes superposition viable, or whether selective routing is fundamentally the only thing that works at this scale.
